# StudioFlow Long-Form Demo Assets

## 5-Minute Interview Walkthrough

### Goal

Balance product storytelling with enough technical signal to make the project feel credible in interviews.

### Recommended Order

1. Product framing
2. Login and onboarding context
3. Dashboard
4. Multi-location switch
5. Calendar and appointment lifecycle
6. Clients, staff, and services
7. Payments and consent forms
8. Notifications and audit logs
9. Public booking
10. Customer manage-booking flow
11. Analytics
12. Architecture and deployment wrap-up

## Screen-By-Screen Script Support

### 1. Product framing

- Show: login page or portfolio summary slide.
- Say: "StudioFlow is a multi-location SaaS platform for service businesses like salons, tattoo studios, barbershops, and wellness practices."
- Highlight: one product, two surfaces: internal operations and customer self-service.

### 2. Login and onboarding context

- Show: sign-in and briefly mention onboarding.
- Say: "The first admin creates the workspace through onboarding, and afterward the system behaves like a normal internal operations product."
- Avoid: walking through every onboarding field unless onboarding itself is the showcase.

### 3. Dashboard

- Show: today’s appointments, revenue snapshot, notifications, recent activity.
- Say: "The dashboard is meant to be operational, not just informational."
- Highlight: calm hierarchy and real backend data.

### 4. Multi-location switch

- Show: location selector in the header.
- Say: "StudioFlow supports multi-location teams without overcomplicating the product model."
- Highlight: location-aware context across views.

### 5. Calendar and appointment lifecycle

- Show: open one appointment drawer.
- Say: "The calendar and appointment drawer are the center of the product because that’s where daily work happens."
- Highlight: status changes, payment context, consent status, activity timeline.

### 6. Clients, staff, and services

- Show: one clean pass through these modules.
- Say: "These modules support the scheduling workflow without feeling like disconnected admin screens."
- Highlight: realistic data, role-aware UI, and operational completeness.

### 7. Payments and consent forms

- Show: payment list and one consent record.
- Say: "I wanted the product to go beyond bookings and cover the operational edges that matter in real studios."
- Highlight: payment tracking and consent readiness.

### 8. Notifications and audit logs

- Show: notification center and audit log page.
- Say: "Notifications make the app feel alive, and audit history adds accountability for multi-user workflows."
- Highlight: unread state, activity history, actor and timestamp context.

### 9. Public booking

- Show: service selection through confirmation.
- Say: "Customers use a separate public flow that stays studio-aware and location-aware."
- Highlight: backend-backed services, staff, availability, and booking reference.

### 10. Customer manage-booking flow

- Show: lookup, then reschedule or cancel.
- Say: "Customers can safely manage their booking without entering the internal dashboard."
- Highlight: booking reference lookup and validation safeguards.

### 11. Analytics

- Show: final summary charts and metrics.
- Say: "Analytics closes the loop by turning operational data into business visibility."
- Highlight: real backend-driven summaries.

### 12. Architecture and deployment wrap-up

- Show: GitHub repo, README, or a short architecture slide.
- Say: "The frontend is built with React and TypeScript, and the backend uses Spring Boot, Spring Security, JWT auth, and studio-scoped authorization."
- Highlight: local/staging/production config, CI workflows, hosted demo, and seeded demo readiness.

## What Not To Spend Time On

- repetitive CRUD edits
- long pauses while typing
- every filter on every page
- deep settings details
- minor empty states

## Closing Lines

### Interview version

"The goal with StudioFlow was to build something that feels like a real SaaS product: role-aware, multi-user, customer-facing where it needs to be, and operationally complete beyond just scheduling."

### Portfolio version

"StudioFlow shows how I approach product-minded full-stack development: strong internal workflows, a clean customer-facing experience, and a realistic deployment and demo story."
