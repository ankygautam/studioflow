# StudioFlow Demo Shot Plan

## Hero Shot

### 01-login-hero

- Show: clean login page with `admin@studioflow.co` and the premium left panel.
- Why it matters: establishes StudioFlow as a polished internal SaaS product immediately.
- Note: if using the hosted demo, let the Render wake-up note remain visible.

### 02-dashboard-overview

- Show: today’s appointments, revenue snapshot, notifications, recent activity.
- Why it matters: proves the dashboard is operational, not decorative.

### 03-location-switch

- Show: top-bar location selector switching between locations.
- Why it matters: quickly communicates multi-location support.

### 04-calendar-core-workflow

- Show: booking calendar with one strong appointment card and schedule density.
- Why it matters: this is the core daily workflow for the product.

### 05-appointment-detail

- Show: appointment drawer with service, staff, payment state, notes, communications, and activity.
- Why it matters: shows workflow depth in one screen.

### 06-clients

- Show: client list and a detailed client record with history context.
- Why it matters: reinforces customer management, not just appointment booking.

### 07-staff-and-services

- Show: staff roster and service catalog with realistic names and pricing.
- Why it matters: shows the workspace is configurable and operational.

### 08-payments

- Show: payment list with status variety and one clear payment record.
- Why it matters: demonstrates operational completeness beyond scheduling.

### 09-consent-forms

- Show: template and submission statuses.
- Why it matters: highlights domain-specific workflow depth.

### 10-notifications

- Show: bell, unread badge, and recent notifications panel.
- Why it matters: gives the product a live operational feel.

### 11-audit-logs

- Show: audit log page or timeline with actor, action, entity, and timestamp.
- Why it matters: shows accountability and admin trust features.

### 12-public-booking

- Show: branded booking flow with service, staff, time, and customer details.
- Why it matters: proves the customer-facing side is a real product surface.

### 13-booking-confirmation

- Show: confirmation with booking reference and location.
- Why it matters: this is one of the strongest “finished product” moments.

### 14-manage-booking

- Show: manage-booking lookup plus reschedule or cancel confirmation.
- Why it matters: demonstrates secure self-service and lifecycle depth.

### 15-analytics-wrap

- Show: analytics page with charts and summary cards.
- Why it matters: closes on business value and operational visibility.

## Recording Notes Per Shot

- Prefer calm cursor movement.
- Keep each shot between 4 and 12 seconds unless it is the main walkthrough.
- Avoid editing inside forms on camera unless it adds product value.
- If a shot includes a drawer, open it before the recording segment starts.
